# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Sanchika serves engineers, designers, and AI coding agents building
compliance-grade interfaces across ComplyEaze, Axal, Pack, future Tools surfaces
at `tools.complyeaze.com`, and external operational SaaS products. They use it
while shaping regulated workflows where source evidence, human review, local
trust boundaries, and accessible operational UI matter more than decorative
novelty.

Primary consumers are product teams adopting shared tokens, primitive contracts,
and compliance patterns without importing app-specific runtime behavior.

## Product Purpose

Sanchika provides contracts and a repeatable agent craft loop for producing
clear, distinctive, trustworthy regulated interfaces. It is not an AI runtime
or automated compliance judgment. It exists to make reusable interface
decisions portable across ComplyEaze first, then Axal, Pack, Tools, and external
operational SaaS adopters, while keeping each product's runtime, auth, data, and
release boundaries separate.

Success means a real ComplyEaze surface can consume Sanchika tokens and
primitives with visible accessibility, state, and review evidence before broader
consumer rollout or public production-readiness claims.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, and evidence-forward.

Sanchika should feel prepared, professionally supported, source-aware, and
trustworthy under pressure. The voice should be direct and operational: name the
state, show the source, expose uncertainty, and make the next safe action clear.

## Anti-references

Sanchika must not look or sound like a generic purple-gradient AI startup,
crypto dashboard, playful consumer app, decorative bento page, or ornamental SaaS
template. It should avoid unsupported claims such as bank-grade, filing-ready,
audit-proof, government-official, CA-verified, or production-ready unless
separate evidence proves them.

It must not become an app framework, route scaffold, backend service,
authentication layer, scraping runtime, Pack extension implementation, or Tools
product template.

## Design Principles

1. Practice boundary honesty: shared design contracts stop at tokens,
   primitives, patterns, and proof surfaces unless a consumer adoption plan
   explicitly expands scope.
2. Make trust visible: source, provenance, uncertainty, review state, permission,
   and local-vs-upload boundaries should be first-class UI material.
3. Optimize for dense professional work: layouts should stay scannable,
   keyboard-operable, and resilient to long company names, identifiers, dates,
   amounts, and document titles.
4. Prefer extraction over invention: reusable patterns should come from real
   ComplyEaze, Axal, Pack, Tools, or external operational SaaS needs instead of
   generic decorative sections.
5. Keep claims evidence-backed: production, accessibility, compliance, and
   official-source claims require verifier, review, or adoption evidence.
6. Treat S7's 20 product-pattern contracts as candidate package grammar, not
   proof of consumer adoption or permission to begin the separate S8 canonical
   gallery showcase rebuild.
7. S7 does not close the five C1 reservations: brand memorability, emotional
   impact, product-specific character, production workflow depth, and
   edge-state completeness all remain open.
8. Keep Sanchika contract-first. One repository-owned Agent Skill may coordinate
   development-time craft outside the package and runtime dependency graph.
9. Retain source visibility, uncertainty, human accountability, accessibility,
   restraint, and Indian relevance while retiring ledger-heavy styling as the
   dominant identity.
10. Permit only finite, proof-oriented motion and interaction feedback. Continue
    to reject global scroll reveals, parallax, infinite decoration, animation
    frameworks, and motion-gated content.

## S10-S11 Ratification

- **D4R:** Runtime packages remain `tokens`, `primitives`, and `patterns` at
  v0.1.1. The canonical `sanchika-craft` skill is development tooling only.
- **D7R:** Source, interpretation, and accountable human decision remain
  visibly distinct. Sanchika's identity may be more expressive, but never at
  the cost of trust boundaries or accessibility.
- **D8R:** Motion is finite and evidence-oriented. Production may use one
  approximately 720ms proof sequence plus ordinary focus, hover, disclosure,
  and confirmation feedback.
- **Craft exception:** S10 permits only the canonical skill, one run template,
  one validator, five calibration controls, one Sanchika run, and the
  gallery-owned identity layer.

Craft protocol versioning begins at `0.1.0` independently of package versions.
Further protocol hardening waits for evidence from Sanchika and two consumers.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Sanchika targets WCAG 2.2 AA for applicable web UI and follows WAI-ARIA Authoring
Practices where native HTML is insufficient. Interactive primitives need
keyboard support, visible focus, accessible names, and non-color-only state
communication.

The system must account for reduced motion, color-vision differences, long
Indian compliance identifiers, mobile and desktop workflows, and users operating
under time-sensitive filing, notice, or evidence-review pressure.
