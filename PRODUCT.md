# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Sanchika serves engineers, designers, and AI coding agents building
compliance-grade interfaces across ComplyEaze, Axal, Pack, and future Tools
surfaces at `tools.complyeaze.com`. They use it while shaping regulated
workflows where source evidence, human review, local trust boundaries, and
accessible operational UI matter more than decorative novelty.

Primary consumers are product teams adopting shared tokens, primitive contracts,
and compliance patterns without importing app-specific runtime behavior.

## Product Purpose

Sanchika is an independent design-system SDK for Indian compliance workflows. It
exists to make reusable interface decisions portable across ComplyEaze first,
then Axal, Pack, and Tools, while keeping each product's runtime, auth, data, and
release boundaries separate.

Success means a real ComplyEaze surface can consume Sanchika tokens and
primitives with visible accessibility, state, and review evidence before broader
consumer rollout or public production-readiness claims.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, and evidence-forward.

Sanchika should feel prepared, professionally supported, source-aware, and
trustworthy under pressure. The voice should be direct and operational: name the
state, show the source, expose uncertainty, and make the next safe action clear.

## Anti-references

Sanchika must not look or sound like a generic purple-gradient AI startup,
crypto dashboard, playful consumer app, decorative bento page, or ornamental SaaS
template. It should avoid unsupported claims such as bank-grade, filing-ready,
audit-proof, government-official, CA-verified, or production-ready unless
separate evidence proves them.

It must not become an app framework, route scaffold, backend service,
authentication layer, scraping runtime, Pack extension implementation, or Tools
product template.

## Design Principles

1. Practice boundary honesty: shared design contracts stop at tokens,
   primitives, patterns, and proof surfaces unless a consumer adoption plan
   explicitly expands scope.
2. Make trust visible: source, provenance, uncertainty, review state, permission,
   and local-vs-upload boundaries should be first-class UI material.
3. Optimize for dense professional work: layouts should stay scannable,
   keyboard-operable, and resilient to long company names, identifiers, dates,
   amounts, and document titles.
4. Prefer extraction over invention: reusable patterns should come from real
   ComplyEaze, Axal, Pack, or Tools needs instead of generic SaaS sections.
5. Keep claims evidence-backed: production, accessibility, compliance, and
   official-source claims require verifier, review, or adoption evidence.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Sanchika targets WCAG 2.2 AA for applicable web UI and follows WAI-ARIA Authoring
Practices where native HTML is insufficient. Interactive primitives need
keyboard support, visible focus, accessible names, and non-color-only state
communication.

The system must account for reduced motion, color-vision differences, long
Indian compliance identifiers, mobile and desktop workflows, and users operating
under time-sensitive filing, notice, or evidence-review pressure.
